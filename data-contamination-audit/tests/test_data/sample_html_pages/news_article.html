Scientists Discover New Species in Deep Ocean

By Jane Smith | March 10, 2023

A team of marine biologists has discovered three previously unknown species during a deep-sea expedition in the Pacific Ocean. The expedition, which lasted six weeks, explored depths exceeding 4,000 meters.

Among the discoveries was a bioluminescent jellyfish with unique defensive mechanisms never before observed in cnidarians. The creature, tentatively named Abyssocyanea luminaris, produces a brilliant blue-green light when disturbed.

The second species, a type of deep-sea worm belonging to the family Siboglinidae, was found thriving near hydrothermal vents. Unlike its relatives, this species appears to have developed a symbiotic relationship with a previously unknown strain of chemosynthetic bacteria.

Dr. Maria Rodriguez, lead researcher on the expedition, stated that these findings highlight how much of the ocean remains unexplored. Less than twenty percent of the ocean floor has been mapped in detail.
